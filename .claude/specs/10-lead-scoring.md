# Spec: Lead Scoring

## Overview
This step adds the "scoring" stage of the DASHR AI roadmap (capture → extraction → enrichment → **scoring** → review/export). Once a card has been parsed (step 05) and, optionally, its company enriched with firmographics/signals (step 07), a seller needs a single product-fit number to triage which leads are worth following up on first. This feature computes an explainable, rules-based `lead_score` (0–100) and a `score_breakdown` for each `VisitingCard`, using the seller's own target-customer calibration (`SellerProfile`, step 06) against the prospect's designation seniority, company size, industry/product fit, and buying-intent signals already captured on the card and its linked `Company`/`CompanySignals`. The dashboard's "Leads" table — currently rendering hardcoded mock scores — is wired to this real score so sellers can sort/filter on actual fit instead of a placeholder.

Scoring is deliberately **not** a new `Lead` entity. `VisitingCard` already carries unused `lead_score` (Numeric), `score_breakdown` (JSONB), and `scored_at` (TIMESTAMPTZ) columns from the original schema (`01-database-setup.md`) — this step is the first to populate them. No new table, and no `org_id` column is added anywhere in this step.

**Amended by `20-field-correction` (Phase 2, done):** the one-shot rule below now has exactly one exception — once a card has been scored, correcting any of that card's fields (see `20-field-correction`) unlocks one free rescore. Every "one-shot"/"no re-score" statement in this file describes the *default* state (no correction since the last score); see that spec's Phase 2 section for the full free-rescore mechanics (`rescore_available`, `_has_correction_since_score`, never billed).

**Amended — scoring v2 rework (planned, not yet implemented):** the flat five-category weighting in "Files to create" below (`designation_score`/`company_size_score`/`industry_fit_score`/`momentum_signal_score`/`remark_signal_score`, `version: "v1"`) is being **replaced** — not extended — by an eight-category, priority-ordered model (`version: "v2"`). Priority order (highest to lowest business value, expressed as each category's max-point budget): (1) remark/note signal, (2) product fitment, (3) role/designation, (4) proximity, (5) expansion signal, (6) revenue growth signal, (7) company size, (8) marketplace rating. See the "v2 criteria" block under "Files to create" for the full replacement spec. This is a forward-only logic change: already-scored cards keep their `version: "v1"` `score_breakdown` forever (immutable once issued, same as every other one-shot scoring rule in this file) — there is no backfill/migration of historical scores.

**v2's definition below already includes a second round of refinements** made before any real implementation shipped, based on further review of six of the eight categories. These are folded directly into "v2 criteria" rather than split into a separate `v3` registry entry — this feature is still pre-launch product definition (no real seller has scored a real lead under v2 yet), so there's no already-scored data whose meaning a spec revision would silently change; the version-registry immutability guarantee (below) is about protecting cards scored by real users once this ships, not about freezing the spec mid-design. Summary of the refinements (full detail under "v2 criteria"):
- **Remark/Intent**: a blank remark now scores `0`, not the category average — a sales rep only writes a handwritten note when a lead seems worth flagging, so "no note" is itself mild negative evidence, not missing data. The product-keyword tier now matches only against the seller's own `product_lines` — matching against the buyer's/enrichment's product text conflated "mentions a similar product" with "expressed interest in the seller's product," which are different things.
- **Product fit**: reworked from a keyword-overlap approach (which actually measured "seller and buyer deal in similar things" — a competitor/supplier signal, not a buyer signal) to a cached AI judgment of whether the buyer's industry/business-type would use the seller's product as an operational input (e.g. an air-compressor-body manufacturer needs a bending machine; a trader/stockist of the same product does not; a buyer in the seller's own industry is more likely a competitor or supplier than a customer).
- **Role/Designation**: `individual_contributor` and "no designation known at all" now both score `0`, not `3`/the average — no meaningful purchasing influence, not just a weak signal.
- **Proximity**: replaced with real aerial (great-circle) distance between geocoded coordinates, instead of a same-state/adjacent-state text bucket.
- **Expansion signal & Revenue growth**: both now read a single AI-generated summary of multiple full news articles (searched as `"{company name} latest news"`, not just the bare name) instead of classifying raw RSS headlines by keyword — this also lets the summary resolve directionality that keyword-matching couldn't (e.g. "acquires" vs. "acquired by/merged into"). A separate share-price lookup (`"{company name} share price"`) computes quarter-over-quarter growth for companies that are actually publicly listed; the common case (a private/unlisted trade-show lead) contributes no extra signal either way. Both categories share one **distress override**: if the summary indicates closure/bankruptcy/being-acquired-into-another-company, or the QOQ share price shows a significant decline, `expansion_signal_score` and `revenue_growth_score` are both forced to `0` — explicit negative evidence overrides whatever tier they'd have otherwise landed on. This override is scoped to exactly these two categories; every other category (remark, product fit, role, proximity, company size, marketplace rating) is computed independently and unaffected by it.
- **Company identity check (applies across the news/share-price step):** a company name alone is ambiguous — a news or share-price search for a generic-sounding name can return results about a same-named but unrelated company, and scoring off the wrong company's news would actively mislead a seller. Before any headline/article is fed into the summary, it's checked against the target company's own known context (city, industry, website domain if any) for a plausible identity match — reusing the disambiguation pattern already proven in `apps/api/app/services/enrichment_providers/local_presence_provider.py` (`_looks_like_same_company_by_url` and friends, built for the identical problem of matching an IndiaMART profile to the right company) rather than inventing a new mechanism. An article that doesn't plausibly match is dropped before summarization, not just down-weighted.

**Amended — versioned scoring & A/B experimentation (planned, not yet implemented):** v1 and v2 are the first two entries in what is now a permanent, general **scoring version registry**, not a one-time migration from an old model to a new one. Since more scoring versions are expected in the future (each one a candidate to A/B test against whichever version is currently "live"), scoring is restructured so that (a) any number of versions can coexist indefinitely, (b) which version scores a given card is a deterministic, configurable experiment assignment rather than always-latest, and (c) a card's score is permanently pinned to whichever version produced it — changing the experiment configuration, shipping a future version, or retiring v1 from new assignments must never alter a single already-scored card. See the new "Scoring versioning & A/B experimentation" section below for the full mechanism; it supersedes the simpler "this is a forward-only logic change" framing above with a permanent architecture rather than a one-off migration note.

## Depends on
- **04 — Visiting card bulk upload**: cards must exist (`visiting_cards`, `upload_batch_id`).
- **05 — Parsing visiting card**: scoring requires a card to have reached `status == "extracted"`, and reuses `designation.classify()` output already stored on `VisitingCard.designation_level`.
- **06 — Company profile backend**: scoring reads `SellerProfile` (`industry`, `product_lines`, `target_customer_description`, `billing_address`) as the fit-calibration input.
- **07 — Data enrichment**: scoring reads `CompanySignals` (`linkedin_employee_count`, `hiring_signal`, `gem_tender_count`, `import_export_activity`, `marketplace_verified_badge`, `udyam_category`, `product_lines_summary`, `indiamart_business_type`, `indiamart_employee_count_band`, `indiamart_annual_turnover_band`, `estimated_revenue_band`, `indiamart_rating`, and — added by this rework, see "Database changes" — `news_summary`, `news_distress_detected`, `share_price_qoq_growth_pct`) and `Company` (`name`, `hq_city`, `hq_country`, `enrichment_status`). Enrichment is **not required** before scoring — a card can be scored right after extraction, with every enrichment-dependent category falling back to that category's average score (see the null-handling rule under "v2 criteria" — remark's blank case and role/designation's unknown case are the two deliberate exceptions that score `0` instead) rather than 0, and re-scored later (once corrected, per `20-field-correction`) to pick up real enrichment data.
- **v2 rework only** — introduces three new external dependencies (none exist in the codebase today):
  1. A **cached AI judgment** for `product_fit_score`, reusing the existing Claude/Anthropic client already wired up for `enrichment_summary.py`'s summarization calls — no new SDK, just a new call site and a new cache table (`ProductFitJudgment`, see "Database changes").
  2. A **free geocoding provider** (OpenStreetMap Nominatim — no API key) for `proximity_score`, converting free-text addresses to lat/lon once and caching the result (`GeocodedAddress`, see "Database changes"), then computing real aerial (haversine) distance.
  3. An extended **news pipeline** for `expansion_signal_score`/`revenue_growth_score`: fetching full article text (not just RSS headlines) for the top Google News results, summarizing them with Claude (same client as above), and a separate share-price lookup/QOQ calculation for publicly-listed companies. This *for this scoring feature's purposes* replaces reading raw-headline keyword classification off `CompanySignals.recent_news_signals` — that field and `news_signal_provider.py`'s original 3-bucket classifier (`funding`/`expansion`/`new_facility`) remain untouched for step 07's own general enrichment/display purposes (whatever else in the product may read them); this feature's scoring categories simply stop being one of their readers, in favor of the new `news_summary`/`news_distress_detected` fields.
  `apps/api/app/services/enrichment_providers/local_presence_provider.py`'s existing company-identity disambiguation pattern (`_looks_like_same_company_by_url` and its neighbors, built originally for IndiaMART profile matching) is reused, not reinvented, for verifying a news article is actually about the target company before it's summarized/scored.

Steps 08 (delete card) and 09 (bulk select/parse/enrich) are unrelated and not required.

## API endpoints (apps/api)

- `POST /cards/{card_id}/score` — score a single card now — org-authenticated (owner or org-admin visibility via `scope_to_visible_users`) — no request body; returns `CardOut` (200, now carrying `rescore_available: bool` per `20-field-correction` Phase 2). Raises `404` if the card isn't visible to the current user, `409` (`CardNotEligibleForScoringError`) if `card.status != "extracted"`, `409` (`CardAlreadyScoredError`) if `card.lead_score` is already set **and no field was corrected since** (see amendment above). Mirrors the existing `POST /cards/{card_id}/enrich-company` single-action CTA pattern exactly — an explicit "Score Card" action, never auto-triggered after parsing or enrichment. **Scoring is one-shot per card by default**: once a card has been scored, it cannot be re-scored, even if it's later enriched with better company data — there is no "Re-score Card" affordance unless the seller first corrects a field. Sellers should enrich a company (step 07) before scoring a card, not after, to get the best score on the one attempt.

- `POST /cards/score` — bulk score a seller-selected set of cards — org-authenticated — request `CardScoreRequest {card_ids: list[UUID], min_length=1}`, response `CardScoreResponse {enqueued_count: int, skipped_count: int}` (200). Cards that aren't visible to the current user, aren't `status == "extracted"`, or are already scored (`lead_score is not None`) are silently skipped and counted, not raised — mirrors `POST /cards/enrich-companies`'s best-effort batch semantics exactly. **Deliberately unchanged by `20-field-correction` Phase 2**: `enqueue_scoring` still skips every already-scored card unconditionally, even one with a correction since its last score — free rescoring is a single-card, drawer-only affordance (correcting a field is itself a drawer-only action), not a bulk one; a documented scope boundary, not an oversight.

Both endpoints enqueue a Celery task per card rather than compute synchronously in the handler, per CLAUDE.md's "bulk/long-running work is a Celery task, not inline in a request handler" and to keep the single- and bulk-card code paths identical (one task, `.delay()`'d once or many times) — see "Background jobs" below.

## Database changes
No database changes for v1. `visiting_cards.lead_score` (Numeric), `visiting_cards.score_breakdown` (JSONB), and `visiting_cards.scored_at` (TIMESTAMPTZ) already exist from migration `0001_initial_schema.py` and are already declared on the `VisitingCard` model — this step is the first to write to them. No new table, no new column, no `org_id` addition (scoring reads `Company`/`CompanySignals`, which remain org-agnostic shared cache tables by existing design from step 07; tenant scoping is enforced only on the `visiting_cards` side via the existing `scope_to_visible_users` helper).

`score_breakdown` JSONB shape written by v1 (unchanged on every card already scored under v1 — immutable once issued):
```json
{
  "designation_score": 0,
  "company_size_score": 0,
  "industry_fit_score": 0,
  "momentum_signal_score": 0,
  "remark_signal_score": 0,
  "total": 0,
  "version": "v1"
}
```
Note: the original draft in `01-database-setup.md` named the fourth field `engagement_score`; this spec renamed it `momentum_signal_score` for clarity (it scores company growth/momentum signals, not card-level engagement) — JSONB has no schema to migrate, so this was a naming decision, not a breaking change.

`score_breakdown` JSONB shape written by **v2** (the rework this amendment specifies — see "v2 criteria" under "Files to create"):
```json
{
  "remark_signal_score": 0,
  "product_fit_score": 0,
  "role_designation_score": 0,
  "proximity_score": 0,
  "expansion_signal_score": 0,
  "revenue_growth_score": 0,
  "company_size_score": 0,
  "marketplace_rating_score": 0,
  "total": 0,
  "version": "v2"
}
```
No new column: `score_breakdown` is still the same untyped JSONB column — the `version` field is what lets `GET /cards/{id}` and the drawer distinguish a v1 row (5 keys) from a v2 row (8 keys) rather than a schema change enforcing one shape.

**v2 rework — new tables/columns needed to support the refined categories.** All additive (nothing removed or renamed from v1's fields), no change to the `score_breakdown` shape above:

- **New table `product_fit_judgments`** (cross-org shared cache, same rationale as `Company`/`CompanySignals` — the underlying question, "does a business of type X in industry Y need product Z," has no seller- or org-specific answer): `judgment_id` (PK), `product_signature_hash` (hash of the seller's normalized `product_lines`/`industry` text — not `seller_id`, so two sellers describing the same product genuinely share a cache hit, same "cache once, reuse" principle as Company), `buyer_industry_normalized`, `buyer_business_type`, `verdict` (`"needs"` / `"partial"` / `"no_need"`), `reasoning` (`Text`, the AI's short explanation, kept for debuggability), `created_at`. No `org_id` — this is reference-data-shaped, not tenant data.
- **New table `geocoded_addresses`** (cross-org shared cache, same rationale): `address_hash` (PK, hash of the normalized address text), `raw_address` (`Text`), `latitude`/`longitude` (`Numeric`, nullable — a failed/ungeocodable lookup is still cached so it isn't retried every score), `geocoded_at`. Reused for both a card's prospect address and a seller's billing address — same cache, same key scheme.
- **`CompanySignals` gains four nullable columns**, populated by the extended news pipeline during company enrichment (not at scoring time — same trigger point as every other `CompanySignals` field): `news_summary` (`Text`, the AI-generated combined summary of the top identity-verified articles), `news_summary_generated_at` (`TIMESTAMPTZ`), `share_price_qoq_growth_pct` (`Numeric`, nullable — stays null for the common case of an unlisted company), `news_distress_detected` (`Boolean`, nullable — true if the summary indicates closure/bankruptcy/being-acquired, or the QOQ figure shows a significant decline; this single flag is what both `expansion_signal_score` and `revenue_growth_score` check for the shared override). `CompanySignals.recent_news_signals` (the existing JSONB list, keyword-classified) is **not removed or repurposed** — it keeps being populated exactly as before for step 07's own general enrichment/display purposes; this scoring feature's categories simply read the new fields instead.

## Scoring versioning & A/B experimentation

**(planned, not yet implemented — the mechanism referenced by the "versioned scoring & A/B experimentation" amendment in Overview.)** v1 and v2 are the first two entries in a permanent **scoring version registry**, not a one-time migration. This section is the general mechanism any future version plugs into — it is not re-written each time a new version ships, only extended.

- **`SCORING_VERSIONS: dict[str, Callable]`** — a module-level registry in `scoring.py` mapping a version string to the function that implements it, e.g. `{"v1": _calculate_score_v1, "v2": _calculate_score_v2}`. The v1 logic under "Files to create" above becomes `_calculate_score_v1`; the v2 logic under "v2 criteria" becomes `_calculate_score_v2`. **Once any card has been scored under a given key, that key's function body is frozen forever** — a bug fix or tweak to `_calculate_score_v2` is a new registry entry, never an in-place edit, because editing it in place would silently change the meaning of every already-persisted `version: "v2"` row without changing its version string. (This guarantee governs the *implementation*, once shipped — it's why v2's definition is still being revised directly in this spec document right now, and won't be once real cards are scored under it; see the Overview amendment above.)
- **`_SCORING_VERSION_ROLLOUT: dict[str, int]`** — a module-level config, e.g. `{"v1": 0, "v2": 100}`, percentages summing to 100, one entry per currently-assignable key in `SCORING_VERSIONS` (a version can exist in the registry but be excluded from the rollout dict entirely once retired from new assignments — its historical cards are unaffected either way). Configurable data, not a hardcoded branch — same rule CLAUDE.md already applies to scoring weights and `PricingRate`.
- **`select_scoring_version(user_id: uuid.UUID) -> str`** — deterministic, stable-hash bucketing: hash `user_id` to a stable integer in `[0, 100)` (e.g. `int(hashlib.sha256(str(user_id).encode()).hexdigest(), 16) % 100`), then walk `_SCORING_VERSION_ROLLOUT` in its (stable, insertion-ordered) key order accumulating percentage ranges until the hash falls in one. The same `user_id` always lands in the same bucket for as long as the rollout config is unchanged — a seller isn't flipped between versions from one card to the next mid-experiment. This is a pure function (no DB access) living in `scoring.py`, unit-testable directly.
- **`calculate_score(card, company, signals, seller_profile, existing_phones, product_fit_verdict, distances_km, *, version: str | None = None) -> dict`** becomes a thin dispatcher: if `version` is passed explicitly, look it up directly in `SCORING_VERSIONS` and call it (used for rescores, below); if `version` is `None` (a fresh, first-time score), call `select_scoring_version(card.user_id)` to pick one, then dispatch the same way. Either path returns whatever shape that version's function produces — the dispatcher itself has no knowledge of any version's internal shape. `product_fit_verdict`/`distances_km` are resolved by the worker before this call and simply ignored by any registry entry (like v1) that doesn't need them.
- **Rescores stay pinned to their original version.** A free rescore (per `20-field-correction`) never re-rolls the experiment assignment — `scoring_processing.py` passes `version=card.score_breakdown["version"]` explicitly on a rescore, so a correction can change a card's *score* but never its scoring *version*. This keeps a single card's `score_breakdown` shape stable across its own lifetime and keeps A/B comparisons clean (a card can't migrate experiment arms mid-life).
- **Immutability guarantee.** Changing `_SCORING_VERSION_ROLLOUT`, adding a new `SCORING_VERSIONS` entry, or removing a version from the rollout must never alter any already-scored card. This isn't new enforcement machinery — it falls out of two invariants already in this spec: (1) the one-shot rule (`CardAlreadyScoredError`) means a card is only ever scored once outside the single-rescore exception, and (2) nothing in this feature ever iterates historical cards and recomputes them in bulk — `select_scoring_version`/`calculate_score` are only ever called from the single- and bulk-score endpoints' Celery task, on cards not yet scored (or freshly correction-unlocked).
- **Analysis.** A card's scoring version is always recoverable via the existing JSONB column — `score_breakdown->>'version'` — with no new column needed. A Postgres expression index on that path is a plausible future optimization if per-version reporting query volume grows, but isn't required for this step. Building an actual per-version score-distribution report is explicitly out of scope here; `16-dashboard-analytics` is the natural future home for that once it exists.

## Background jobs
- **New task: `app.workers.scoring_processing.score_card_task(self, card_id: str)`** — added to `celery_app.py`'s `include` list. Loads the `VisitingCard` plus its linked `Company`/`CompanySignals` (if any) and the scoring user's `SellerProfile`, calls `scoring.calculate_score(...)`, and writes `lead_score`, `score_breakdown`, `scored_at` onto the card. Follows the exact conventions already established by `enrich_company_task`/`process_card`: `bind=True`, `max_retries=3`, manual `self.retry(countdown=2**self.request.retries)`, `db = SessionLocal(); try/finally: db.close()`. Unlike enrichment, there is no in-flight status to guard against (`card.status` does not change during scoring — no external I/O, so no "scoring" intermediate state is needed); the retry-safety guard instead re-checks `card.status == "extracted"` on every attempt (fresh delivery or retry) and skips as stale if the card's status changed underneath it (e.g. deleted, merged) mid-retry.
- **Trigger**: enqueued only from `POST /cards/{card_id}/score` (single) and `POST /cards/score` (bulk) — never auto-chained after `process_card` or `enrich_company_task` complete, consistent with every existing pipeline stage being a distinct, seller-initiated action.

**v2 rework only:**
- **`enrich_company_task` gains a new step** (company-level, same trigger point as every other `CompanySignals` field): search `"{company name} latest news"`, fetch the top article pages, filter to ones that plausibly identity-match the target company (via the reused `local_presence_provider.py` pattern), summarize the surviving articles with Claude, and separately look up `"{company name} share price"` for a QOQ figure when the company is listed. Writes `news_summary`, `news_summary_generated_at`, `share_price_qoq_growth_pct`, `news_distress_detected` onto `CompanySignals` — additive alongside the existing `recent_news_signals` write, which is unchanged. This keeps the expensive network/AI work at enrichment time, off the scoring hot path, same as every other signal.
- **`score_card_task` gains two new *pre*-`calculate_score` steps**, both seller/buyer-specific (not company-level, so they can't be precomputed at enrichment time the way news can): (1) a cache-then-Claude lookup for the buyer's `(seller_product_signature, buyer_industry, buyer_business_type)` product-fit verdict against `product_fit_judgments`, writing a fresh row on a cache miss; (2) a cache-then-geocode lookup for both the buyer's and seller's addresses against `geocoded_addresses`, writing fresh rows on a cache miss. Both resolved values are passed **into** `calculate_score(...)` as plain arguments — `calculate_score` itself stays a pure function with no DB/API access (see "Rules for implementation"); the worker does the I/O first, exactly the same pattern already used for `existing_phones`.

### v2 criteria (replaces v1 above — the scoring rework this amendment specifies)

Same file (`apps/api/app/services/scoring.py`), same "pure function, module-level constants" contract — `calculate_score()` gains `existing_phones: list[str]` (the card's own `CardPhone.phone_e164`/`phone_raw` values), `product_fit_verdict: str | None`, and `distances_km: dict[str, float | None]`, all resolved and passed in by the worker (see "Background jobs") — `calculate_score` itself does no I/O. 8 categories, **strictly priority-ordered by max-point budget** (100 total): 24 / 20 / 16 / 12 / 10 / 8 / 6 / 4.

**Null-handling rule (applies to every category below, with two explicit exceptions):** a category whose underlying signal is genuinely *unavailable* (no linked `Company`, no `CompanySignals` row, enrichment still pending, blank free-text field, unparseable region, etc.) scores that category's **average** (`max // 2`), never `0`. `0` (or another low, explicit tier) is reserved for a signal that *was* evaluated and came back negative/no-match/weak — "we don't know" and "we checked and it's bad" must never collapse to the same number. **The two exceptions**, described in items 1 and 3 below, are deliberate: a blank remark and an unknown/`individual_contributor` designation are both treated as mild negative evidence rather than missing data, based on how these specific signals behave in practice for a B2B industrial sales rep filling out a card.

1. **`remark_signal_score` (max 24, priority 1 — highest)** — tiered scan of `VisitingCard.special_remark`, highest matching tier wins:
   - Tier 1 (24): contains a positive-intent keyword ("interested", "important", "urgent", "budget", "decision", "follow up", "next week") **or** contains a keyword overlapping the **seller's own** `SellerProfile.product_lines` (a mention of the seller's product) — deliberately checked only against the seller's product list, not `signals.product_lines_summary` (the buyer's own product text), since matching the buyer's product text there was measuring "the remark mentions something like what the buyer already sells," not "the remark mentions the seller's product."
   - Tier 2 (16): contains a phone-number-shaped token (regex/parser over common Indian mobile/landline formats) that is **not** already present in `existing_phones` — i.e. the remark surfaces a genuinely new contact number.
   - Tier 3 (8): any other non-empty remark (present but matches neither tier above).
   - **Null/blank `special_remark`: `0`, not average.** A sales rep only writes a handwritten note when a lead seems worth flagging — a blank note is itself mild negative evidence, not an unknown.

2. **`product_fit_score` (max 20, priority 2)** — reads `product_fit_verdict` (resolved by the worker via `product_fit_service.get_or_judge_fit`, cached per `(seller_product_signature, buyer_industry, buyer_business_type)` — an AI judgment of whether a business of the buyer's type/industry would use the seller's product as an operational input, not whether they deal in similar products):
   - `"needs"` (clear operational need — e.g. an air-compressor-body manufacturer needs a bending machine): 20.
   - `"partial"` (plausible but not a clear operational need): 12.
   - `"no_need"` (buyer deals in similar/competing products, or has no operational use for it — e.g. a trader/stockist of the seller's own product category, or a buyer in the seller's own industry who's more likely a competitor or supplier than a customer): 0 — an explicit negative, since the question was actually asked and answered, not missing data.
   - `product_fit_verdict is None` (no linked company, seller has no product/industry description to judge against, or the AI call itself failed): avg = 10.
   - Deliberately does **not** read `Company.name`/`CompanySignals.product_lines_summary`/`VisitingCard.products_offered` for keyword overlap — matching on "deals in similar products" measures a competitor/supplier relationship, not a buyer relationship, which is exactly what this criterion needs to distinguish.

3. **`role_designation_score` (max 16, priority 3)** — keyword scan of `VisitingCard.job_title` for a purchase/commercial function, checked **before** falling back to seniority:
   - Purchase/procurement/buying/commercial/sourcing/vendor-development keyword match: 16 (highest — the actual budget-holder for a B2B industrial sale).
   - No function match, fall back to `VisitingCard.designation_level`: `c_level`=13, `director`=10, `manager`=6.
   - **`individual_contributor`: `0`** — no meaningful purchasing influence, not just weak.
   - **No function match and no `designation_level` at all: `0`** — treated the same as `individual_contributor` rather than as missing data, since the absence of any seniority signal on a B2B industrial card correlates with low purchasing influence more often than with "we just don't know."

4. **`proximity_score` (max 12, priority 4)** — real aerial (great-circle/haversine) distance between geocoded coordinates, via `distances_km` (resolved by the worker through `geocode_service.get_or_geocode`, cached):
   - Buyer-side point: `VisitingCard.address`, falling back to `Company.hq_city`.
   - Seller-side point: `SellerProfile.billing_address`. `SellerProfile.target_regions` is **not** used here — it's typically a multi-region free-text preference ("Maharashtra, Gujarat"), not one geocodable point, so it doesn't reduce to a single coordinate; it stays on the profile for display/future use but doesn't feed this calculation.
   - Distance tiers: ≤50km: 12. ≤200km: 9. ≤500km: 5. >500km: 1.
   - Either point fails to geocode (blank/unparseable address, or the geocoding provider returns nothing — including a cached "we tried and failed" result): avg = 6.

5. **`expansion_signal_score` (max 10, priority 5)** — reads `CompanySignals.news_summary`/`news_distress_detected` (populated by the extended news pipeline during enrichment — see "Background jobs"):
   - **Distress override (checked first):** `news_distress_detected` true (the summary indicates closure/bankruptcy/being-acquired-into-another-company, or `share_price_qoq_growth_pct` shows a significant decline — see "Rules for implementation" for the shared threshold) → **0**, regardless of anything else in the summary.
   - Otherwise: summary indicates real expansion (new plant/facility, the company itself acquiring another, a funding round) → 10. Summary has other business news but nothing expansion-related → 3.
   - `news_summary` null (no news enrichment yet, or no identity-matched articles were found for this company): avg = 5.

6. **`revenue_growth_score` (max 8, priority 6)** — same `news_summary`/`news_distress_detected` source:
   - **Distress override (checked first):** same `news_distress_detected` flag as above → **0**.
   - Otherwise: summary indicates revenue growth (record revenue, turnover up) → 8. Summary present but no revenue-growth mention → 2.
   - `news_summary` null: avg = 4.
   - Deliberately excludes a website-derived signal (`website_signal_provider.py` remains stub-only). The dedicated share-price QOQ lookup does **not** independently move this score for the common case of an unlisted company — `share_price_qoq_growth_pct is None` contributes nothing extra; it's only checked for the distress override above when it does exist and shows a significant decline.

   **Company-identity verification (applies to both 5 and 6):** before any article is fed into the Claude summary call, it's checked for a plausible identity match against the target company's own known context (HQ city, industry, website domain) using the same disambiguation approach already proven in `local_presence_provider.py` for IndiaMART profile matching — an article that doesn't plausibly match the target company is dropped before summarization, not merely down-weighted. This applies to the share-price lookup too: a same-named-but-different listed company must not be treated as a match.

7. **`company_size_score` (max 6, priority 7)** — combines an employee-count band and a turnover band into a 0–4 base score (new threshold table, mirrors v1's `_EMPLOYEE_COUNT_BAND_THRESHOLDS` shape), plus bonus points:
   - Employee signal: `signals.indiamart_employee_count_band` if present, else `signals.linkedin_employee_count` (reuse v1's banding), else `signals.udyam_category`.
   - Turnover/"GST band" signal: `signals.indiamart_annual_turnover_band` if present, else `signals.estimated_revenue_band`.
   - Base score from the stronger of the two available bands (0–4); bonus `+1` if `signals.import_export_activity` is true, `+1` if `signals.gem_tender_count and signals.gem_tender_count > 0` — capped at 6 total.
   - No `CompanySignals` row at all (company never enriched): avg = 3.

8. **`marketplace_rating_score` (max 4, priority 8 — lowest)** — `signals.indiamart_rating` (0–5 scale) rescaled to 0–4 (`round(rating / 5 * 4)`). Deliberately IndiaMART-only — `CompanySignals.google_rating` already exists on the model but per this update's instructions is explicitly deferred ("we will add Google rating later"), so it is **not** read despite being available.
   - `indiamart_rating` null: avg = 2.

`total` = sum of all 8 components (0–100 by construction, since every branch — including every avg fallback and both explicit-zero exceptions — stays within its category's max). `version: "v2"`.

## Rules for implementation
- Every query touching `visiting_cards` filters through the existing `scope_to_visible_users` helper against `VisitingCard.user_id` — do not add a new `org_id` column to `visiting_cards`, `companies`, or `company_signals`; follow the org-scoping deviation already established in step 01 (`users.org_id`/`role`, not a literal column on every table)
- No raw SQL string interpolation — SQLAlchemy query builder or bound params only
- All scoring weights, bands, and keyword lists live in `scoring.py` as module-level data, never as inline branches in `routers/cards.py` or `workers/scoring_processing.py`
- Scoring computation itself (`calculate_score`) is a pure function — no DB writes, no Celery/session imports inside `scoring.py`; only the Celery task and service layer touch the database
- Bulk scoring is always a Celery task per card, never synchronous in the request handler, even though the computation itself is cheap — this keeps the single-card and bulk-card code paths identical and leaves room for slower scoring signals (e.g. an LLM-based qualitative fit pass) to be added later without changing the API contract
- `score_card` never changes `VisitingCard.status` — `scored_at` being non-null is the only signal that a card has been scored, exactly as `Company.enrichment_status` (not `VisitingCard.status`) is the signal for enrichment completion
- Scoring is never auto-triggered after parsing or enrichment completes — it is always a seller-initiated action via the `/score` endpoints
- **Scoring is one-shot per card, enforced server-side, not just hidden in the UI.** `score_card_now` and `enqueue_scoring` both reject/skip a card whose `lead_score` is already set (`CardAlreadyScoredError`, 409, for the single-card endpoint; silently skipped+counted for bulk) — a client cannot bypass the "no re-score" rule by calling the API directly even though the UI never exposes a re-score control
- The dashboard/"Leads" page (`apps/web/app/dashboard/page.tsx`) never initiates scoring — no bulk or per-row scoring CTA exists there. All scoring is initiated from the Upload page (bulk button + per-row icon) or the card detail drawer's one-shot "Score Card" button
- API contracts are the Pydantic models (`CardScoreRequest`/`CardScoreResponse`, extended `CardOut`/`CardDetailOut`) — the TS types in `apps/web/lib/api.ts` are hand-aligned to match, never assumed

**Versioning & A/B rework only:**
- A new scoring version is always a **new** `SCORING_VERSIONS` entry, never an edit to an existing entry's function body once any card has been scored under that key — editing a live version's logic in place would silently change the meaning of every historical `score_breakdown` row carrying that `version` string without the string itself changing. (Until then — pre-launch, no real card scored yet — "v2" itself is still the spec's live working definition, per the Overview amendment.)
- `_SCORING_VERSION_ROLLOUT` is configurable module-level data (percentages summing to 100), the same rule this file already applies to every weight/band/keyword table — never hardcoded branch logic in the router or worker
- `select_scoring_version(user_id)` is called exactly once per **initial** score event, from `scoring_processing.py`, and nowhere else — it is never called again for a rescore and never called from a batch/report job
- A free rescore (`20-field-correction`) always reuses the version already stored on `card.score_breakdown["version"]` and passes it explicitly to `calculate_score(..., version=...)` — it never re-derives or re-rolls the experiment assignment, so a correction can change a card's score but never its scoring version
- Changing `_SCORING_VERSION_ROLLOUT`, adding a new `SCORING_VERSIONS` entry, or removing a version from the rollout must never trigger recomputation of any already-scored card — nothing in this feature iterates historical cards through `calculate_score` in bulk; scoring only ever runs from the single-/bulk-score endpoints' Celery task against not-yet-scored (or freshly correction-unlocked) cards

**v2 rework only:**
- The 8-category priority order (remark > product fitment > role/designation > proximity > expansion > revenue growth > company size > marketplace rating) is expressed **only** through each category's max-point budget (24/20/16/12/10/8/6/4) in `scoring.py` — never as a runtime branch that skips or short-circuits a lower-priority category. Every category is still computed and written into `score_breakdown` on every score — "priority" changes how much a category can move the total, not whether it's evaluated
- A missing/unavailable signal scores that category's average (`max // 2`), never `0`, **except the two documented exceptions** (blank remark; unknown/`individual_contributor` designation) — those two treat "no signal present" as itself mild negative evidence, not missing data. Every other category's avg-fallback behavior is the general rule: never conflate "we don't know" with "we checked and it's bad"
- The remark's phone-number tier compares candidate numbers against the card's own `CardPhone` rows passed in as `existing_phones` — it never re-derives phone numbers from `card.raw_ocr_text` or treats every digit-heavy substring as a phone number; it's a narrow "is this number already on file for this card" check, not a general-purpose phone extractor
- Function-keyword lists, distance-tier thresholds, and every other weight/band/keyword table for these categories all live in `scoring.py` as module-level data, same rule as every other weight/band/keyword list in this file
- `_calculate_score_v2` stays a pure function like every other registry entry — the product-fit verdict and the geocoded distances are resolved by `scoring_processing.py` **before** calling `calculate_score`, exactly like `existing_phones` already is; `_calculate_score_v2` itself makes no DB queries, no Claude calls, no HTTP calls
- **The distress override is scoped to exactly `expansion_signal_score` and `revenue_growth_score`** — `CompanySignals.news_distress_detected` must never zero out or otherwise affect `remark_signal_score`, `product_fit_score`, `role_designation_score`, `proximity_score`, `company_size_score`, or `marketplace_rating_score`; those six are computed completely independently of the news pipeline's findings
- A "significant" QOQ share-price decline (the threshold that sets `news_distress_detected`) is a module-level constant in whatever service computes it (e.g. `_SIGNIFICANT_DECLINE_THRESHOLD_PCT = -10`), not a magic number inline — same rule as every other threshold in this file
- **Every news article and share-price match must pass the company-identity check before being used** — reusing `local_presence_provider.py`'s existing disambiguation pattern, never a bare company-name string match. An article/listing that doesn't plausibly match the target company is dropped, not down-weighted, before it ever reaches the Claude summary call or the QOQ calculation
- `product_fit_service.get_or_judge_fit` and `geocode_service.get_or_geocode` are both cache-first, mirroring CLAUDE.md's existing "never call an enrichment provider per-request without checking the cache first" rule — a cache miss writes its result (including a `None`/failure result) before returning, so an ungeocodable address or an unresolvable product-fit pair isn't re-attempted on every subsequent score
- `product_fit_judgments` and `geocoded_addresses` are cross-org shared caches (no `org_id`), matching the existing `Company`/`CompanySignals` precedent — the underlying question each answers ("does industry X need product Y," "where is this address") has no tenant-specific answer
- `score_breakdown["version"]` is what distinguishes a v1 row (5 keys) from a v2 row (8 keys) on already-persisted cards — frontend code reading `score_breakdown` must handle both shapes generically (see "Files to change" above), never assume the 8-key v2 shape unconditionally
- v2 is a **forward-only** logic change relative to v1: it must never trigger recomputation of any card that was already scored under v1 — only newly-scored (or, per `20-field-correction`, newly free-rescored) cards get a `version: "v2"` breakdown
